Interpreting the FWI Prediction

The Forest Weather Index (FWI) represents the potential risk of forest fires based on weather conditions. The FWI value is calculated using a combination of temperature, humidity, wind speed, and rainfall, among other factors. A higher FWI value indicates a greater risk of forest fires, while a lower value suggests a lower risk. The FWI scale can be interpreted as follows:

  • Low (0 - 5): Minimal fire risk; conditions are not conducive to wildfires.
  • Moderate (5 - 20): Fire risk is present, but conditions are still manageable.
  • High (20 - 40): Significant fire risk; fires may spread rapidly under current conditions.
  • Very High (40 - 60): Severe fire risk; fires are likely to spread very quickly and require immediate action.
  • Extreme (60+): Extreme fire danger; fires will spread uncontrollably, requiring urgent and extensive firefighting resources.

Understanding this scale allows you to interpret the risk level and take the necessary precautions for fire prevention and control.
